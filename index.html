20. sponsored| rationale |composition

发表于 2025-10-29 更新于 2025-10-30 分类于 English ， Vocabulary ， 20220306

sponsored

adjective — supported financially by a company, organization, or individual

中文释义: 赞助的；由公司、组织或个人提供资金支持的。
Examples:

  • The event was sponsored by a major technology company.
  • She attended a sponsored conference on environmental issues.

rationale

noun — the reasons or logical basis for a belief, decision, or action

中文释义: 原理；基本理由；逻辑依据。
Examples:

  • The rationale behind the new policy was explained in the meeting.
  • Can you explain the rationale for choosing this method?

composition

noun — the way in which something is put together or arranged

中文释义: 组成；构成；结构。
Examples:

  • The composition of the team has changed since last year.
  • This painting shows excellent composition and balance.

noun — a piece of writing, music, or art created by someone

中文释义: 作品；作文；创作。
Examples:

  • The students wrote a short composition for homework.
  • Beethoven’s piano compositions are still widely performed today.

inheritance

noun — money, property, or possessions that are passed down from someone after they die

中文释义: 遗产；继承物。
Examples:

  • She received a large inheritance from her grandmother.
  • Inheritance laws vary from country to country.

noun — the process of passing on qualities or traits from one generation to another

中文释义: 遗传；继承的特性。
Examples:

  • Eye color is often determined by genetic inheritance.
  • Scientists study inheritance patterns in families.

redundancy

noun — the state of being no longer needed or useful, often resulting in job loss

中文释义: 多余；冗余；裁员。
Examples:

  • The company announced redundancies due to restructuring.
  • There’s some redundancy in the report that could be removed.

noun — the use of words or information that is unnecessary or repetitive

中文释义: 冗余；重复。
Examples:

  • Avoid redundancy in your essay to make it concise.
  • The sentence has redundancy and can be simplified.

fascination

noun — a strong interest or attraction for someone or something

中文释义: 着迷；极大兴趣。
Examples:

  • He has a fascination with ancient history.
  • The child watched the magician’s tricks with fascination.

aircraft

中文释义: 飞行器；航空器（包括飞机、直升机等）。
Examples:

  • The aircraft took off smoothly despite the strong wind.
  • Modern aircraft are designed to use less fuel and reduce noise.

rapport

noun — a friendly and harmonious relationship in which people understand each other well

中文释义: 融洽的关系；和谐的沟通（指人与人之间相互理解、信任和共鸣）。
Examples:

  • The teacher built a strong rapport with her students.
  • Good rapport between team members improves cooperation.
  • When I joined the agency, I hit it off with my colleagues immediately. I still get on well with them and there’s a very good rapport between us. This is all part of my job satisfaction.

originality

noun — the quality of being new, unusual, and creative rather than copied from something else

中文释义: 独创性；原创性；新颖独特。
Examples:

  • The originality of her artwork impressed everyone at the exhibition.
  • Good writing often combines clarity with originality.
  • I work in advertising. I love my work! It’s really rewarding and stimulating – satisfying and interesting. Originality and creativity are very important in this industry, of course; we have to come up with – produce – lots of new ideas.

presentation

noun — a talk or speech in which you explain or show something to an audience

中文释义: 演讲；报告；展示（通常指在观众面前介绍某个主题或成果）。
Examples:

noun — the way in which something is shown, arranged, or designed

中文释义: 呈现方式；外观；表现形式。

client contact

中文释义: 客户接触；客户沟通（指与客户交流或维系关系的行为）。

Examples:

中文释义: 政府机构；行政部门。

  • The Environmental Protection Agency enforces pollution laws.

sense of achievement

中文释义: 成就感；完成某事后的自豪或满足感。
Examples:

  • Finishing the marathon gave her a great sense of achievement.
  • You’ll feel a real sense of achievement once the project is complete.

no sense at all

中文释义: 毫无道理；完全讲不通；没有意义。

15. red tape| bureaucracy |chained to a desk

发表于 2025-10-29 分类于 English ， Vocabulary ， 20220325

red tape

noun — official rules and processes that seem unnecessary and cause delays

中文释义: 繁文缛节；官僚手续（指不必要的行政程序或规定导致拖延）。
Examples:

  • The project was delayed for months because of government red tape.
  • We need to cut through the red tape to get this approved faster.

bureaucracy

noun — a system of government or management with many complicated rules and officials

中文释义: 官僚体制；繁杂的行政体系。
Examples:

  • The new policy got stuck in the layers of bureaucracy.
  • Many people complain that too much bureaucracy slows down innovation.

noun — the officials or administrators who work in such a system

中文释义: 官僚机构；官僚人员。
Examples:

  • The bureaucracy decided to reject the proposal.
  • He left the public sector because he was tired of dealing with bureaucracy.

chained to a desk

idiom — forced to spend all your working time sitting at a desk, usually in an office

中文释义: 被困在办公桌前；整天坐在办公室里工作。
Examples:

  • I don’t want a job where I’m chained to a desk all day.
  • He felt chained to his desk, with no time for outdoor activities.

14.perks| allowance |technically

发表于 2025-10-29 分类于 English ， Vocabulary ， 20220325

perks

noun — special benefits or advantages that come with a job or position

中文释义: 额外津贴；特殊待遇；附加福利。
Examples:

  • One of the perks of working here is free lunch every day.
  • Company cars and health insurance are common employee perks.

allowance

noun — an amount of money regularly given to someone for a specific purpose

中文释义: 津贴；补助；零用钱。
Examples:

  • She gets a monthly allowance from her parents.
  • The company provides a travel allowance for business trips.

noun — a permitted amount or acceptable limit of something

中文释义: 限额；允许量。
Examples:

  • The baggage allowance on this flight is 20 kilograms.
  • There’s a small allowance for human error in the system.

technically

adverb — according to the exact meaning, rules, or facts

中文释义: 严格来说；根据确切定义；从技术上讲。
Examples:

  • Technically, you’re not allowed to park here.
  • The system is technically outdated but still functional.

adverb — relating to the practical skills or knowledge in a particular field

中文释义: 技术上地；在技术方面。
Examples:

  • She’s technically skilled in software engineering.
  • The film was impressive, both artistically and technically.

中文释义: 想出；提出（主意、计划、答案等）。

  • The group campaigned to raise awareness about climate change.

10.centimeter | indicate |diagram

发表于 2025-10-28 更新于 2025-10-29 分类于 English ， Vocabulary ， 20220325

centimeter

verb — a unit for measuring length; one hundredth of a meter (written abbreviation: cm)

中文释义: 厘米；长度单位，为一米的百分之一。
Examples:

  • The table is about 80 centimeters high.
  • She measured the fabric and found it was 120 centimeters long.

indicate

verb — to show that something is true or exists

中文释义: 表明；显示；说明某事为真或存在。
Examples:

  • The results indicate that the treatment is effective.
  • A survey indicates a growing interest in online education.

verb — to point to or direct attention to something

中文释义: 指出；指向。
Examples:

  • The sign indicates the entrance to the museum.
  • She indicated the correct answer on the chart.

verb — to make a signal or gesture showing what you mean or intend to do

中文释义: 示意；打信号（如车辆打转向灯）。
Examples:

  • He indicated left before turning the car.
  • She nodded to indicate that she understood.

diagram

noun — a simple drawing that shows how something works or what parts it contains

中文释义: 图表；示意图；结构图。
Examples:

  • The teacher drew a diagram on the board to explain the process.
  • This diagram shows the layout of the new office.

verb — to represent something in a diagram

中文释义: 用图示表示；画出……的示意图。
Examples:

  • The structure of the machine is diagramed below.
  • The data can be diagramed to show the relationship between variables.
