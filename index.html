11. sense of achievement| no sense at all |hit it off with

发表于 2025-10-29 分类于 English ， Vocabulary ， 20220325

sense of achievement

noun — a feeling of pride or satisfaction when you have done something well or successfully

中文释义: 成就感；完成某事后的自豪或满足感。
Examples:

  • Finishing the marathon gave her a great sense of achievement.
  • You’ll feel a real sense of achievement once the project is complete.

no sense at all

phrase — completely illogical, unreasonable, or without meaning

中文释义: 毫无道理；完全讲不通；没有意义。
Examples:

  • What he said made no sense at all to me.
  • It makes no sense at all to spend so much money on that.

hit it off with

idiom — to quickly become friendly or have a good relationship with someone

中文释义: 一拍即合；与某人很快相处融洽。
Examples:

  • I really hit it off with my new colleagues at the office.
  • They met at a party and immediately hit it off with each other.

11. red tape| bureaucracy |chained to a desk

发表于 2025-10-29 分类于 English ， Vocabulary ， 20220325

red tape

noun — official rules and processes that seem unnecessary and cause delays

中文释义: 繁文缛节；官僚手续（指不必要的行政程序或规定导致拖延）。
Examples:

  • The project was delayed for months because of government red tape.
  • We need to cut through the red tape to get this approved faster.

bureaucracy

noun — a system of government or management with many complicated rules and officials

中文释义: 官僚体制；繁杂的行政体系。
Examples:

  • The new policy got stuck in the layers of bureaucracy.
  • Many people complain that too much bureaucracy slows down innovation.

noun — the officials or administrators who work in such a system

中文释义: 官僚机构；官僚人员。
Examples:

  • The bureaucracy decided to reject the proposal.
  • He left the public sector because he was tired of dealing with bureaucracy.

chained to a desk

idiom — forced to spend all your working time sitting at a desk, usually in an office

中文释义: 被困在办公桌前；整天坐在办公室里工作。
Examples:

  • I don’t want a job where I’m chained to a desk all day.
  • He felt chained to his desk, with no time for outdoor activities.

11.perks| allowance |technically

发表于 2025-10-29 分类于 English ， Vocabulary ， 20220325

perks

noun — special benefits or advantages that come with a job or position

中文释义: 额外津贴；特殊待遇；附加福利。
Examples:

  • One of the perks of working here is free lunch every day.
  • Company cars and health insurance are common employee perks.

allowance

noun — an amount of money regularly given to someone for a specific purpose

中文释义: 津贴；补助；零用钱。
Examples:

  • She gets a monthly allowance from her parents.
  • The company provides a travel allowance for business trips.

noun — a permitted amount or acceptable limit of something

中文释义: 限额；允许量。
Examples:

  • The baggage allowance on this flight is 20 kilograms.
  • There’s a small allowance for human error in the system.

technically

adverb — according to the exact meaning, rules, or facts

中文释义: 严格来说；根据确切定义；从技术上讲。
Examples:

  • Technically, you’re not allowed to park here.
  • The system is technically outdated but still functional.

adverb — relating to the practical skills or knowledge in a particular field

中文释义: 技术上地；在技术方面。
Examples:

  • She’s technically skilled in software engineering.
  • The film was impressive, both artistically and technically.

中文释义: 图表；示意图；结构图。

9.exploit | prime |gap-filling

exploit

verb — to use something well in order to gain as much from it as possible

中文释义: 充分利用；开发；运用。
Examples:

  • We need to exploit renewable energy sources more efficiently.
  • The team is keen to exploit new opportunities in the market.

verb — to treat a person or situation as an opportunity to gain an advantage for yourself (disapproving)

中文释义: 剥削；利用他人以谋私利（含贬义）。
Examples:

  • The company was accused of exploiting child labour.
  • He exploited his friend’s trust to get money.

verb (computing) — to use a weakness in a computer system to gain access or cause damage

中文释义: （计算机）利用系统漏洞攻击或入侵。
Examples:

  • Hackers tried to exploit a security flaw in the website.
  • The virus exploited a bug in the operating system.

noun — a brave, exciting, or unusual act or adventure

中文释义: 英勇行为；冒险事迹。
Examples:

  • The film tells the exploits of a daring pilot.
  • His military exploits earned him several medals.

prime

adjective — most important; main

中文释义: 主要的；首要的。
Examples:

  • The prime reason for the failure was poor communication.
  • Safety is our prime concern.

adjective — of the best quality; excellent

中文释义: 最佳的；优质的。
Examples:

  • This restaurant serves prime beef.
  • He’s in the prime years of his career.

noun — the time in your life when you are strongest, most successful, or most active

中文释义: 全盛时期；黄金期。
Examples:

  • She’s still in her prime as an athlete.
  • Many singers retire long before their prime.

verb — to prepare someone or something for a particular situation or purpose

中文释义: 使准备好；事先告知；做好准备。
Examples:

  • The coach primed the team for the final match.
  • She primed herself with a list of possible interview questions.

noun (mathematics) — a number that can be divided only by itself and 1

中文释义: （数学）素数；只能被 1 和自身整除的数。
Examples:

  • 2, 3, 5, and 7 are all prime numbers.
  • Prime numbers play an important role in modern cryptography.

gap-filling

noun — an exercise in which you fill in missing words to complete sentences or texts

中文释义: 填空练习；补全句子练习（常用于语言学习）。
Examples:

  • The teacher gave us a gap-filling exercise to test vocabulary.
  • Gap-filling tasks help learners improve their grammar.

noun — something done temporarily to fill a shortage or absence

中文释义: 临时补缺；过渡性措施。
Examples:

  • The part-time role was just a gap-filling job until she found full-time work.
  • The company introduced a gap-filling policy during the transition period.
