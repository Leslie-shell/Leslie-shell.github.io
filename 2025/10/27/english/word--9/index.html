9.exploit | prime |gap-filling

发表于 2025-10-27 更新于 2025-10-28 分类于 English ， Vocabulary ， 20220325

exploit

verb — to use something well in order to gain as much from it as possible

中文释义: 充分利用；开发；运用。
Examples:

  • We need to exploit renewable energy sources more efficiently.
  • The team is keen to exploit new opportunities in the market.

verb — to treat a person or situation as an opportunity to gain an advantage for yourself (disapproving)

中文释义: 剥削；利用他人以谋私利（含贬义）。
Examples:

  • The company was accused of exploiting child labour.
  • He exploited his friend’s trust to get money.

verb (computing) — to use a weakness in a computer system to gain access or cause damage

中文释义: （计算机）利用系统漏洞攻击或入侵。
Examples:

  • Hackers tried to exploit a security flaw in the website.
  • The virus exploited a bug in the operating system.

noun — a brave, exciting, or unusual act or adventure

中文释义: 英勇行为；冒险事迹。
Examples:

  • The film tells the exploits of a daring pilot.
  • His military exploits earned him several medals.

prime

adjective — most important; main

中文释义: 主要的；首要的。
Examples:

  • The prime reason for the failure was poor communication.
  • Safety is our prime concern.

adjective — of the best quality; excellent

中文释义: 最佳的；优质的。
Examples:

  • This restaurant serves prime beef.
  • He’s in the prime years of his career.

noun — the time in your life when you are strongest, most successful, or most active

中文释义: 全盛时期；黄金期。
Examples:

  • She’s still in her prime as an athlete.
  • Many singers retire long before their prime.

verb — to prepare someone or something for a particular situation or purpose

中文释义: 使准备好；事先告知；做好准备。
Examples:

  • The coach primed the team for the final match.
  • She primed herself with a list of possible interview questions.

noun (mathematics) — a number that can be divided only by itself and 1

中文释义: （数学）素数；只能被 1 和自身整除的数。
Examples:

  • 2, 3, 5, and 7 are all prime numbers.
  • Prime numbers play an important role in modern cryptography.

gap-filling

noun — an exercise in which you fill in missing words to complete sentences or texts

中文释义: 填空练习；补全句子练习（常用于语言学习）。
Examples:

  • The teacher gave us a gap-filling exercise to test vocabulary.
  • Gap-filling tasks help learners improve their grammar.

noun — something done temporarily to fill a shortage or absence

中文释义: 临时补缺；过渡性措施。
Examples:

  • The part-time role was just a gap-filling job until she found full-time work.
  • The company introduced a gap-filling policy during the transition period.
